Clam Chowder

Description

Clam chowder is any of several chowder soups in American cuisine containing clams. In addition to clams, common ingredients include diced potatoes, salt pork, and onions.

Ingredients:

  • 6 strips thick cut bacon
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  • 2 ribs celery
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 tbsp hot sauce
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/3 cup flour
  • 3 cups Half and Half
  • 8 oz. clam juice
  • 1 chicken bouillon cube
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 1/4 lbs. potatoes
  • 3 chopped clams
  • Fresh parsley & freshly cracked black pepper

Steps:

  1. Cook the bacon over low heat. Once the bacon is cooked, set aside on a paper towel lined plate. Chop once cooled. Reserve 2 tbsp of bacon drippings.
  2. Add the reserved bacon drippings and butter to the pot over medium heat. Add the diced onions and celery. Toss to coat and soften for 5-6 mins. Add the garlic, hot sauce, Worcestershire sauce, and seasonings. Cook for 1 min.
  3. Add the flour and cook for 2 mins stirring continuously.
  4. Add the chicken broth in small splashes, stirring continuously. Add the half and half.
  5. Stir in the chicken bouillon and add the bay leaf. Add the juice from the canned clams to the soup. Stir in the additional clam juice.
  6. Bring the soup to a gentle boil, then reduce to a simmer. Simmer for 20 mins, partially covered. Stir occasionally.
  7. Peel and dice the potatoes and add them to the soup. Bring back to a gentle bubble and let the potatoes cook for 20-25 mins, until fork tender. Reduce heat to low.
  8. Stir in the clams and let them heat through, about 5 mins. Remove bay leaf.
  9. Serve and garnish with parsley, black pepper, and chopped bacon.
